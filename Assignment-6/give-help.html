Anyone can experience suicide risk, but some groups are at higher risk in Vermont.

36%

36% of LGBTQ+ high school students made a suicide plan vs. 9% of their peers.

5x

Adults with a disability are 5x more likely to seriously consider suicide.

3x

Men are 3x more likely to die by suicide than women. 29.3 vs 7.1

42%

42% of VT adults store a firearm at home. Firearms are involved in more than 1/2 of suicide deaths.

#BeThe1To promotes five action steps to help you communicate with someone who may be thinking of suicide.

1. Ask directly if they are thinking about suicide, then listen without judgment. If someone says they are, call 1-800-273-8255 or 9-1-1.

2. Keep people safe by removing access to potentially dangerous household items like firearms or medications.

3. Be there. Make an emotional connection. Let them know that you care.

4. Help them connect to support. Ensure they have hotline numbers programmed into their phone or written where they can easily find them.

5. Follow up with texts, calls or face-to-face visits.

Early prevention can take on many forms. Here are some examples of what it looks like.

  1. Talking to someone you haven’t heard from in a while.

  2. Connecting with community and peer support programs.

  3. Learning to recognize the warning signs.

  4. Not shying away from talking about suicide.

  5. Storing firearms locked and unloaded.

  6. Supporting community center development.

  7. Participating in after school programs.

Limiting access to potentially dangerous things can save lives.

Lock to Live can help you make decisions about temporarily reducing access to potentially dangerous things. BeSMART teaches you how to keep kids safe by properly storing firearms.
