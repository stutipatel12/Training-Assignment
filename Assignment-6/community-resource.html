Improving mental wellness for people in the LGBTQ+ community.

LGBTQ+ people are not at increased risk due to their gender identity or sexual orientation in and of itself, but experience increased risk for suicide, specifically because of mental health issues and substance abuse due to lower levels of acceptance and belonging in the broader community.

Having a mental illness, a history of alcohol or drug misuse, social isolation, and societal prejudice and discrimination can increase the risk of suicide for people in the LGBTQ+ community.

To reduce suicide risk, individuals and organizations can work towards building more societal and family acceptance, create places for connectedness, and improve access to competent mental health care.

Source: Preventing Suicide in LGBTQ+ communities, AFSP
